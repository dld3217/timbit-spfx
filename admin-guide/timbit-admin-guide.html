Tim·bit History — Admin Guide

Last updated: July 2026  ·  For questions contact Dennis Dodd

What is Tim·bit? A weekly HPE Networking sales enablement archive maintained by Tim Vanevenhoven. Each week Tim publishes 2–3 bite-sized resources — videos, case studies, blog posts, solutions — that SEs and TMs can share with customers. As an admin, your job is to add those entries, publish them, and send the weekly email to the distribution list.

Overview

The Tim·bit History web part lives on the EnterpriseSalesTeamHome SharePoint site. It has two views:

  • Public (Archive view) — visible to all site members; shows published entries with search and filters
  • Admin view — only visible to users listed in the Admin Emails property; adds the dark nav bar at the top with Add/Edit Entries, Generate Email, Import History, and Admin Guide tabs

Admin Access

Admin access is controlled by the Admin Emails field in the web part property pane. Any user whose HPE email is listed there will see the admin nav bar.

1
Navigate to the Tim-Bits page on EnterpriseSalesTeamHome
2
Click Edit on the page (top right)
3
Click the web part → click the two parallel lines icon (property pane)
4
Under Admin Access, add the email address (comma-separated)
5
Click Republish
Admin emails are case-insensitive but must match the user's HPE login email exactly.

Click Add / Edit Entries in the admin nav bar to open the entry form.

Keep the Body field focused on the sales angle — why should an SE share this with a customer? What problem does it solve?

Publishing Entries

New entries are saved as drafts by default (Published = Off). Drafts are visible to admins in the archive with a yellow "Draft" badge but hidden from regular users.

To publish, open the entry in Add / Edit Entries, toggle Published to On, and click Save.

🖼️
Screenshot placeholder
Entry form showing the Published toggle in the On position
Once published, the entry immediately appears in the public archive. The "This Week" highlighted section at the top of the archive automatically shows entries from the most recent Week Of date.

Generating the Weekly Email

Click Generate Email in the admin nav bar. The tab automatically loads all published entries from the most recent Week Of date and renders a preview of the formatted HTML email.

🖼️
Screenshot placeholder
Generate Email tab showing the compose fields (To/CC/BCC/Subject) and the email preview below

The compose fields are pre-filled:

  • To — distribution list email (set in property pane)
  • CC — optional, blank by default
  • BCC — optional; Tim typically puts himself here and moves the DL to BCC to suppress recipient visibility
  • Subject — auto-generated as Tim·bit — Week of YYYY/MM/DD

Sending the Weekly Email

After reviewing the preview, adjust the To/CC/BCC/Subject fields as needed and click ✉ Send.

1
Confirm the correct entries appear in the preview (they should match the current week)
2
Verify the To field has the distribution list address
3
Add yourself to BCC if you want a copy for confirmation
4
Click ✉ Send — you will see "Email queued — it will be sent shortly!"
5
The email is delivered within 1–2 minutes via Power Automate
How it works: clicking Send writes the email details to a SharePoint list (TimBitEmailQueue). A Power Automate flow watches that list and sends the formatted HTML email automatically.

Import History

The Import History tab allows bulk-importing entries from the standalone HTML Tim·bit history file. This is primarily used for one-time migration of historical entries and is unlikely to be needed again.

Paste into the text area and click Preview to see how many entries will be imported vs skipped
4
Click Import — entries already in the system (matched by title) are skipped automatically

Troubleshooting

Changes not showing after save

SharePoint caches aggressively. Do a hard refresh: Ctrl+Shift+Delete → clear cached images and files → Ctrl+Shift+R.

Email preview shows "No published entries found"

Make sure at least one entry for the current week is published (Published toggle = On) and that the Week Of date matches across all entries for that week.

"Failed to queue email" error on Send

The TimBitEmailQueue SharePoint list or the Power Automate flow may be unavailable. Check that the flow HPEN Tim·bit — Send Weekly Email is turned On in Power Automate (flow.microsoft.com).

Admin nav bar not visible

Your email is not in the Admin Emails property pane field. Ask an existing admin to add your HPE email address.

New entries not appearing in "This Week" section

Check that the Week Of field on your new entry matches exactly (YYYY/MM/DD) with the most recent week's entries. A mismatch will push the entry into the archive instead.
